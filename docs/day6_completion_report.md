## Executive Summary

Day 6 focused on building the mathematical core of the platform: the **Financial KPI Engine** (`kpi_engine.py`). This engine connects to the SQLite Data Warehouse and systematically computes 20 critical financial, valuation, and growth metrics for every company. 

The output, stored in `kpi_summary.csv`, aggregates the most recent annual data for 92 baseline companies and standardizes historical time-series data to extract growth signals (Revenue Growth, EPS Growth, Market Cap Growth). The engine enforces strict data-quality boundaries, gracefully handling zero-division risks and missing values.

---

## Engine Architecture & KPI Definitions

The engine executes a 3-stage process:
1. **Extraction**: Fetches the complete temporal datasets from `profitandloss`, `financial_ratios`, `balancesheet`, `cashflow`, and `market_cap`.
2. **Growth Computation**: Identifies the latest available chronological year and calculates YoY percentage change using the formula `(Current - Prev) / Abs(Prev)`. This method accurately handles negative base values (e.g., negative profits recovering to positive).
3. **Derived Consolidation**: Computes derived ratios (PEG, Current Ratio) and flattens the output to one row per company.

### 📊 The 20 Computed Metrics
| Category | Metrics Included |
|----------|------------------|
| **Growth Metrics** | Revenue Growth, PAT Growth, EPS Growth, Cash Flow Growth, Market Cap Growth, 5-Year CAGR |
| **Profitability** | ROE (Return on Equity), ROCE (Return on Capital Employed), Operating Margin, Net Margin |
| **Liquidity & Solvency** | Debt Equity, Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Interest Coverage |
| **Valuation** | PE (Price to Earnings), PB (Price to Book), PEG (PE to Growth), Dividend Yield, Enterprise Value |
| **Cash Flow** | FCF (Free Cash Flow) |

> [!NOTE]  
> **Data Assumptions Applied:**
> - **Quick Ratio**: Due to the absence of granular Inventory data in the current datasets, Quick Ratio acts as a proxy mirroring Current Ratio.
> - **Current Ratio**: Estimated strictly as `other_asset / NULLIF(other_liabilities, 0)`.
> - **PEG Ratio**: Defaults to `NaN` if EPS Growth is negative or zero, as PEG ratios become mathematically misleading during profit contractions.
